Research & Projects

Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) Chatbot

> What is it

A ChatGPT-style chatbot I created and deployed for the company I interned at. I developed the system using state-of-the-art techniques such as Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) and Low-Rank Adaptation LLM fine-tuning. The app was deployed into production with Docker.

> Why I made it

To make the usage of complex semiconductor testing equipment easier for engineers at the company. With the chatbot, engineers were able to get instant answers and instructions about equipment use.

> Tech stack: Python, Pytorch, HuggingFace, Docker, FastAPI, Streamlit

Vision for Robotic Cooking Assistant

> What is it

Implementing computer vision algorithms, specifically pose estimation, for a robotic assistive system designed to help people with cooking tasks. The robot takes in voice commands and hands the requested object back to the person.

> Why I'm making it

This project is part of my master's thesis. The aim is to add vision capabilities to the robot and research if pose estimation will improve the robot's grasping accuracy.

> Tech stack: Python, Pytorch, OpenCV, Docker, ROS

Note: the project is still in progress, I cannot share the repo and experimental setup yet. For now, enjoy the image of our robot decorating a Christmas tree.

Parallel Image Compression Algorithm

> What is it

Parallelized image compression algorithm for high-resolution medical images based on the Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT).

> Why I made it

The main aim of the project was to learn how to parallelize large matrix operations and how to write memory-efficient code. This is very important when writing multi-GPU (or multi-node) training code for ML model training, and this project helped me understand data-distributed parallel training.

> Tech stack: C++, Python, OpenMP, OpenMPI

Note: the image above is an example of 4k medical images that were compressed using this algorithm. (source)

Trajectory Prediction for Autonomous Vehicles

Volunteer Experience

Formula SAE (Rensselaer Motorsport)

I spent most of my undergraduate degree doing this one thing only - racecar.

> What is it

Formula SAE (FSAE) is an inter-collegiate engineering design competition where student teams from around the world build formula-style race cars designed for autocross. The teams are judged by vehicle design, performance in dynamic events, and business presentation.

> What I did

Aviation Museum Volunteer (Monino, Russia)

> What is it

The Monino Aviation Museum, located 1.5 hours outside Moscow, holds a unique collection of aircraft on the grounds of an old airfield. The aircraft in the museum is maintained and restored by a large group of volunteers unaffiliated with the museum. These volunteers come from different towns, work in various industries, and are united by their passion for aviation history.

> What I did

I volunteered on the TU-144 aircraft electrical team, maintaining the electrical systems. This is the flagship aircraft in the museum that started the volunteer movement many years ago. It has a fully restored cabin (cockpit and passenger) and, most importantly, live electrical systems - retractable nose, winglets, cockpit/cabin lights, and landing/takeoff lights.
